DownTown

Logan Utah was founded in 1859 on the east side of Cache Valley in northern Utah. There are many historic buildings in Logan that are available for tours. Come see downtown Logan and visit this amazing city!

Logan Temple

The Logan temple broke ground in 1877 and was dedicated 7 years later in 1884. This iconic temple built by The Church of Jesus Christ of Latterday Saints, brought many people to Cache Valley. It has a rich history of the early saints and their trek across the United states.

Utah State University

Another staple of Logan is Utah State University which is located at the mouth of Logan Canyon. This university provides many people valuable knowlegdge and information. It is a beutiful place to visit and to walk around. Many people admire this campus during the spring and the fall with nearby mountains and wildlife that comes alive in the spring and changes beautiful colors during the fall.

Logan Tabernacle

This tabernacle is another beautiful building built by the saints of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. Tours would take place within this amazing building, but it is currently under construction and is not currently taking any tours.

Logan Canyon

Logan Canyon is the perfect place to relax and even camp. This amazing canyon has a river running through a good portion of the canyon. Many people love to go fishing, rock Climbing, or hiking along the canyon. It is most popular during the fall as the leaves change colors and you can drive through this gorgeous view.
